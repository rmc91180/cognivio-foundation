Why we built Cognivio

We have seen firsthand how much thoughtful feedback can change a teacher’s practice. We have also seen how difficult it is for schools and teacher-training programs to provide that feedback consistently.

Observation takes time. Supervisors are stretched thin. Travel, schedules, forms, and documentation get in the way. Great coaching moments are often missed, and too many teachers receive feedback long after the moment when it could have helped most.

Cognivio was built to change that.

Our platform brings together smart technology and human expertise to make classroom observation more frequent, more focused, and more useful. Technology helps organize evidence, surface important moments, and connect classroom practice to the goals and rubrics that matter. Educators bring the judgment, context, care, and professional experience that make feedback meaningful.

We do not believe technology should replace educators. We believe it should help them do their best work.

We support the people who support teachers.

Cognivio is built for administrators, instructional coaches, university supervisors, and teacher-training leaders who want every educator to receive better feedback, more often, from the people best equipped to guide their growth.

For administrators

More visibility into real classroom practice, clearer patterns across teams, and more consistent support for teacher growth.

For teacher-training programs

A practical way to observe clinical practice, support student teachers, and track competency growth across placements.

For supervisors and coaches

Less time lost to manual documentation and more time focused on professional judgment, relationships, and next steps.

Our secret sauce is the combination.

Cognivio works best because it maximizes what technology can do well and what experienced educators can do well. One organizes the evidence. The other understands the person, the classroom, the context, and the path forward.

What we believe

Our work is grounded in a few simple beliefs about education, feedback, and professional growth.

Teaching is deeply human.

Feedback only matters when it reflects context, relationships, professional trust, and the lived reality of the classroom.

Great feedback should not be rare.

Teachers and student teachers deserve more than occasional observation and generic comments.

Leaders need better support.

Administrators and supervisors want to coach well. They need tools that reduce friction instead of adding another burden.

Technology should serve educators.

The platform should strengthen human expertise, not move it to the margins.

Let’s build a better feedback cycle for teachers.
